Project Details

First Data Set:

Dof-summary-of-neighborhood-sales-for-manhattan-for-class-1-2-and-3-family-homes-2009-1

Report Visualization Summary: This Power BI report presents a summary of a dataset by visually categorizing the data into different relationships. The relationships include the median sales price based on the types of homes, such as 1, 2, or 3-family homes, the number of properties available in each neighborhood based on their sales price, and the average sales price by neighborhood. The report also allows for filtering by neighborhood and provides an opportunity to address questions related to the data.

Insights:

  • Upper East Side (79-96) is the most expensive neighborhood in terms of real estate with a median sales price of $2.5 million for 1 family home.
  • 1-family homes are sold the most with a number of sales of 55 and a median price of $7.5 million which is higher than 2 and 3-family homes.
  • 'HARLEM-CENTRAL' has noticeably more TOTAL NO. OF PROPERTIES.
  • 'UPPER WEST SIDE (79-96)' and 'EAST VILLAGE' have noticeably more LOWEST SALE prices for NUMBER OF SALES 2 and TYPE OF HOME '02 FAMILY HOMES'.
  • '01 FAMILY HOMES' has noticeably more HIGHEST SALE PRICE for NUMBER OF SALES 2.
  • 'LITTLE ITALY' has noticeably more AVERAGE SALE PRICE for TYPE OF HOME '03 FAMILY HOMES'.

Second Data Set:

Earned_Income_Tax_Credit__New_York_City_EITC__Claims__Full-Year_Resident_Totals__by_Size_of_Earned_Income

Report Visualization Summary: The Power BI report provides a summary of a dataset by visually organizing the data into different relationships. It displays the total credit amount claimed by tax year and the size of earned income, as well as the average credit earned by those with 0, 1, 2, or 2+ qualifying children. The report allows filtering by year.

Insights:

  • In the year 2008, there was a dip in the total credit amount claimed.
  • Earned income of '$8,001 - $12,000' has a relatively steady percentage of the total Credit Amount Claimed (1 Child).
  • Earned income of '$24,001 - $28,000' and '$20,001 - $24,000' have noticeably more Credit Amount Claimed (2 Children) for Number of Claims (0 Children) 0.
  • Earned income of '$4,001 - $8,000' and '$8,001 - $12,000' have noticeably more Credit Amount Claimed (0 Children) for Tax Year 2013.
  • Earned income of '$4,001 - $8,000' and '$8,001 - $12,000' have noticeably more Credit Amount Claimed (0 Children) for Tax Year 2012.
  • The credit Amount Claimed (2 Children) is trending upward for Tax Year 2014.
